About us

The Value in Health Economics and Policy group is interdisciplinary, cross-faculty, multi-institutional and stakeholder driven. This allows us to effectively and collaboratively research a range of topics with the shared purpose of adding value to people’s health and healthcare systems.

Who we are

We are primarily a research group based in the Newcastle Business School and the University of Newcastle (UON), focusing on issues related to decision-making and policy-making in healthcare. In order to create value across the whole system, we need people researching and practicing across all these different levels. Therefore, our team not only includes classical (health) economic experts, but also researchers from public and allied health, social science, psychology and more. Our members include staff and students of the University of Newcastle across the Faculties of Business and Law, but also Health and Medicine, Arts and Humanities, Science, and Engineering and Built Environment and we welcome new members from all Faculties and Schools. Our membership is also not exclusive to UON and we have both local and global members from other Universities, research institutions and industries. See contact information below. Although our group is composed mostly of academics, the research and industry experience of our members covers a wide range of areas, as indicated by the breadth of schools and faculties our members are from.

What we do

As a group we will identify inefficiencies within the current healthcare system, by examining the literature and coordinating with healthcare stakeholders, to provide sustainable, value-based assessments of health interventions and policies. We do this by investigating not only the costs and benefits but also the decision-making processes of all involved; healthcare practitioners, patients and policy makers. This stakeholder engagement will be facilitated through the organisation and development of workshops, seminars, and educational programmes for both students and external stakeholders, such as members from our local and nearby health districts. These programmes will occur in conjunction with a number of group driven research projects to improve our understanding of health related issues and policy implementation.

Goal

To add more value to healthcare systems from which all stakeholders can benefit.

Vision

To improve effectiveness and efficiency across multiple stakeholder levels by providing evidence-based and patient-focused research and education to students,  researchers, health providers, practitioners and policy makers.

Mission

To draw and extend upon the existing research and policies of value based healthcare by fostering a collaborative, multi-disciplinary, local-global environment which encourages novel strategies to address real world health problems by maximising the skills, expertise and resources of the university and its affiliates, and creates lasting positive impact.
